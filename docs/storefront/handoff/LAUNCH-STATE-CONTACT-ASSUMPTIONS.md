# Launch-State Contact Assumptions

This document defines what public contact paths the Skinfluence storefront can safely present before final support inboxes, care-routing workflows, and intake systems are fully locked.

It exists to answer one practical question:

**What contact language can the storefront use right now without overpromising operational readiness?**

## Core rule

Until the final launch operations are confirmed, the storefront should present contact paths conservatively.

That means:

- public contact language should be clear but limited
- the storefront should not imply a fully mature clinical-support system if one is not yet confirmed
- the storefront should not collapse every question into one generic inbox without distinction

## Safe public contact categories

The storefront can safely present support for:

- billing questions
- shipping questions
- account-access questions
- general product questions
- general evaluation-process questions

These are the categories that fit the generic storefront-support path.

## Cautious categories

The storefront should treat these more carefully:

- treatment-related concerns
- clinically sensitive questions
- irritation or side-effect questions
- care-guidance questions

These should not be framed as ordinary general-support topics unless the real care workflow supports that.

## Recommended public language

The safest public wording is:

- use support for billing, shipping, account, and general questions
- use the appropriate care-support path for treatment-related concerns

This preserves clarity without making claims about a more mature support workflow than currently exists.

## Contact-form assumption

If the Shopify contact form remains in use during launch preparation, assume:

- it is a general support intake
- it is not itself the clinical guidance channel
- it may collect treatment-related concerns only as escalations, not as proof that the workflow is fully routed

## Evaluation-page contact assumption

Before Bask is connected, `/pages/start-evaluation` can safely direct users to:

- FAQ
- Support
- How It Works

It should not promise a live clinical intake if that final step is not yet connected.

## Footer and global contact assumption

If a public support email or contact path is shown in the footer or support page, assume it is valid for:

- general storefront support
- non-clinical account questions

Do not let footer language imply that the same channel is automatically the correct path for medically sensitive treatment concerns.

## What should remain undecided for now

Until operations are locked, the storefront does not need to publicly promise:

- final care-support ownership
- response-time guarantees
- exact escalation workflow
- exact clinical-support inbox structure

Those can be added later once they are operationally real.

## Storefront rule for copy and design work

Until final launch operations are confirmed:

- keep contact language calm and precise
- prefer “support” over over-specific promises
- preserve the distinction between general support and treatment-related concerns
- avoid writing copy that assumes Bask or care workflows are already fully active
