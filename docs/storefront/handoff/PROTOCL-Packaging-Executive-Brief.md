# PROTOCL Packaging Executive Brief

## Objective
Create packaging for PROTOCL that feels premium enough for prestige beauty retail while remaining credible in a clinic, dermatologist office, or spa setting.

The packaging should live comfortably between:

- Nordstrom beauty
- luxury spa retail
- premium clinical skincare

## Current Direction

### Format
- Slim cylindrical airless treatment pump bottle
- Matching tall rectangular carton

### Finish
- Matte black / near-black packaging
- Soft, controlled low-luster surface
- Premium, understated presentation

### Tone
- Clinical but elegant
- Minimal but expensive
- Editorial, not flashy
- Luxurious without feeling decorative

## Front-of-Pack Philosophy

### Bottle
The bottle should carry the more complete product story:

- icons at top
- descriptor in the middle
- dose and active details below
- PROTOCL logo at bottom

Current direction:

- the front-of-pack icon language is being guided by the Midjourney concept imagery rather than the original Claude SVG icon set
- those icons should be treated as the preferred visual reference for packaging only
- they should later be redrawn cleanly in vector once the front-of-pack system is finalized

### Carton
The carton should feel cleaner and more restrained than the bottle:

- icons at top
- descriptor in the middle
- PROTOCL logo at bottom

## What Makes This Feel Premium
The premium feeling will come less from adding more design and more from executing the right details well.

Priority factors:

- strong silhouette
- disciplined hierarchy
- deep matte black finish
- clean spacing
- excellent print precision
- thoughtful material choices

## Premium Touches To Explore

### 1. Soft-touch carton finish
The carton should feel velvety, tactile, and expensive, not slick or generic.

### 2. True matte black execution
The black should read deep and refined, not cheap, dusty, or overly glossy.

### 3. Restrained metallic detail
If used, metallic should be very limited and subtle.

Best use cases:

- small logo treatment
- fine divider
- tiny accent detail

Avoid bold metallic coverage or flashy foil.

### 4. Selective spot gloss or UV
Optional and only if subtle.

Potential uses:

- logo emphasis
- icon row
- tiny structural line

### 5. Highly disciplined typography
The design relies on precision. Small text and minimal layout need to print sharply.

### 6. Refined custom icon language
The icon row should feel distinctive and premium, not generic UI. The current preferred direction is the icon style that emerged in the Midjourney packaging imagery.

That icon language should later be:

- cleaned up
- redrawn in vector
- normalized into a reproducible packaging icon set

## What To Avoid

- high-gloss overall finish
- loud luxury cues
- cluttered front panels
- wellness clichés
- cheap-looking black coatings
- excessive metallic shine

## Strategic Goal
The finished package should feel like:

- a premium anti-aging treatment
- a credible clinic-grade product
- a luxury skincare object someone wants to keep on a vanity

This is not just “nice packaging.” It should feel authoritative, elevated, and premium at first glance and in hand.
