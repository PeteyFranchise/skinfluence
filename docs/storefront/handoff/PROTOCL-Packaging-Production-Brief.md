# PROTOCL Packaging Production Brief

## Purpose
This document is for the printer and packaging production partner translating the PROTOCL concept direction into production-ready packaging recommendations and specifications.

## Packaging System

### Primary Format
- Slim cylindrical airless treatment pump bottle
- Clean stepped pump/cap structure
- Matching tall rectangular secondary carton

### Positioning
The product should feel credible in:

- premium dermatologist offices
- spa/clinic retail
- prestige beauty retail

The packaging should read as luxury clinical skincare, not mass-market wellness and not generic med-spa private label.

## Visual Direction

### Color
- Primary package color: deep matte black / near-black
- Supporting neutrals: off-white, soft gray, warm light neutral
- Optional future accent direction: highly restrained burnished bronze or verdant detail

### Typography
- Display/brand direction: Bodoni Moda-inspired serif elegance
- Supporting information direction: compact, modern sans serif
- Front hierarchy should remain restrained and calm

## Front Panel Hierarchy

### Bottle
Preferred front hierarchy:

1. Small icons at top
2. Product descriptor in middle
3. Dose block below
4. Active ingredients list below
5. PROTOCL logo anchored at bottom

Icon note:

- the current preferred front-of-pack icon language is based on the Midjourney packaging concept imagery, not the earlier Claude SVG icon set
- those icons should be treated as concept reference only until they are rebuilt as production-ready vector artwork

### Carton
The carton should remain cleaner than the bottle:

1. Small icons at top
2. Product descriptor in middle
3. PROTOCL logo at bottom

Avoid crowding the carton front with too much repeated bottle information.

## Recommended Material And Finish Exploration

### 1. Soft-touch carton stock or lamination
Priority recommendation.

We want a tactile finish that feels velvety, smooth, and expensive in hand. The carton should not feel slick, waxy, or commodity-grade.

Desired result:

- rich tactile hand feel
- soft matte surface
- elevated luxury-clinical impression

### 2. True matte black execution
Priority recommendation.

We want a black that feels deep and controlled with subtle dimensionality under light, not a glossy or plastic-looking black.

Desired result:

- deep matte black
- refined low sheen
- premium controlled reflectivity

### 3. Restrained metallic detail
Optional, use sparingly.

If metallic is explored, it should be subtle and limited.

Suggested uses:

- very small logo detail
- fine divider or rule
- tiny accent mark

Avoid:

- bright yellow gold
- mirror foil
- large metallic fields
- flashy luxury cues

### 4. Spot UV / spot gloss
Optional and only if subtle.

If recommended, use only in small, selective areas to create contrast rather than shine.

Best candidates:

- logo
- icon row
- small structural line

### 5. Print precision and tolerance
Critical.

The design direction uses:

- small text
- minimal hierarchy
- fine spacing
- restrained iconography

Production must support:

- crisp small typography
- clean registration
- sharp edges
- no muddy fill
- no fuzzy micro-details

### 6. Production-ready icon redraw
The current icon direction is visually approved at the concept level, but should be rebuilt as a final vector set before print production.

Production partner should expect:

- front-of-pack icon art to be delivered as final vector files later
- consistency of line weight, spacing, and alignment across icons
- a packaging-specific icon set that matches the approved concept direction

## Secondary Panel Expectations
Side and back panels should feel structured and premium, even when carrying regulatory or formula information.

Secondary panels should be:

- organized
- grid-based
- breathable
- highly legible
- visually disciplined

Do not allow the back-of-pack to visually collapse into generic dense copy blocks if a cleaner hierarchy can be maintained.

## Finishes To Avoid

- overall high-gloss laminate
- cheap reflective black coatings
- excessive emboss/deboss for decoration only
- novelty textures
- large metallic coverage
- crowded front-panel messaging

## Questions For The Production Partner
Please advise on:

1. Best substrate and coating combination for a premium matte black carton
2. Whether soft-touch lamination or coating is the stronger route for this packaging system
3. How to achieve subtle controlled sheen without losing the matte look
4. Whether spot UV adds value or risks cheapening the direction
5. Tolerances and recommendations for small front-panel typography and icon detail
6. Durability considerations for maintaining premium matte appearance through handling and shipping

## Production Intent
The finished package should feel:

- premium
- minimal
- authoritative
- tactile
- expensive
- suitable for prestige retail and clinic environments

The goal is not decorative beauty packaging. The goal is premium clinical-luxury packaging with refined material credibility.

## Working Notes
Current front-of-pack formula and actives are provisional and may change after chemist review. Please treat the present hierarchy and finish direction as the main locked concepts, while product-specific copy remains subject to formulary confirmation.

Also note:

- the Midjourney-generated front icon style is currently the preferred packaging reference
- it is not yet final production art
- it should be translated into clean vector packaging artwork in a later step
