# Portland Homepage Cleanup Checklist

This checklist is for turning the Portland trial theme into a Skinfluence-ready homepage.

Current Portland audit:
- the theme feels visually closer to the desired Skinfluence direction
- the typography and spacing are better than the current custom theme
- but the homepage is still mostly Portland demo commerce scaffolding

Observed problems in the Portland preview:
- generic collection cards
- generic product names, vendors, and prices
- product grids and shopping-first modules
- fashion/editorial demo content unrelated to Skinfluence
- headline hierarchy that still underplays the provider-reviewed anti-aging message

## Objective

Strip Portland down to a clean homepage shell, then rebuild it as a content-first, evaluation-led Skinfluence landing page.

## Step 1: Remove Demo Commerce Sections

Disable or remove these homepage sections first:

- collection card rows
- featured collection blocks
- popular items / product carousels
- product vendor and price sections
- any demo banner copy unrelated to Skinfluence
- fashion/streetwear/editorial placeholder copy

Do not worry about replacing them immediately.
The goal is to get to a clean base.

## Step 2: Keep the Structural Winners

Keep if they still look good:

- header shell
- general typography system
- spacing defaults
- the hero section framework
- footer shell

These are the main reasons Portland is promising.

## Step 3: Rebuild the Hero

Use Portland’s hero section, but replace the content with:

Eyebrow:
- `Provider-reviewed anti-aging care`

Headline:
- `Prescription-led anti-aging care for women who expect more from skincare.`

Body:
- `A provider-reviewed monthly treatment designed to improve texture, tone, and fine lines through a more elevated, more consistent experience.`

Primary CTA:
- `Start Evaluation`
- link to `/pages/start-evaluation`

If Portland’s hero supports only one CTA, that is acceptable for now.

## Step 4: Add a Compact Trust Section

Use a multi-column text or icon-list style section with:

- `Provider-reviewed care`
- `Premium monthly treatment`
- `Delivered to your door`
- `Ongoing maintenance support`

Keep it compact.

## Step 5: Add the Core Problem Section

Add a text-led section with:

Headline:
- `When your routine stops feeling worth it.`

Body:
- `There comes a point when another serum, another cream, and another expensive routine stop feeling like progress. Skinfluence is for women who want a more serious anti-aging approach without turning skincare into a second job or navigating the friction of traditional dermatology.`

## Step 6: Add Premium Anti-Aging Explanation

Use an image-with-text or split-content section:

Headline:
- `Premium anti-aging care, built around provider review.`

Body:
- `Skinfluence is a prescription-led monthly treatment designed for women seeking visible improvement in skin quality. If approved, you receive a provider-reviewed anti-aging formula in a streamlined maintenance model designed to support smoother-looking texture, more even-looking tone, and softer-looking fine lines over time.`

CTA:
- `Start Evaluation`

## Step 7: Add How It Works

Use a 3-step process section:

1. `Complete your evaluation`
   - `Answer a few questions about your skin, goals, and history.`

2. `Receive provider review`
   - `A licensed provider reviews your information and determines whether treatment is appropriate.`

3. `Begin monthly care if approved`
   - `If approved, your treatment is delivered to your door through a simple monthly maintenance model.`

## Step 8: Add Benefits / Who It’s For

Use cards, columns, or icon-list style sections.

Possible items:
- `Already invest in skincare or aesthetic maintenance`
- `Want a more advanced anti-aging routine`
- `Prefer guidance over trial and error`
- `Value results, simplicity, and consistency`

or

- `Smoother-looking texture`
- `More even-looking tone`
- `Softer-looking fine lines`
- `More refined overall skin quality`
- `A less tired-looking complexion`

## Step 9: Add Why It’s Different

Use a darker editorial or text-led statement band:

Headline:
- `More guided than skincare. Easier than traditional dermatology.`

Body:
- `Skinfluence is not built like a crowded product line or a generic prescription checkout. It is designed as a premium, provider-reviewed care experience for women who want stronger anti-aging support in a format they can actually stay consistent with.`
- `This is not about adding more products. It is about moving into a more effective anti-aging routine with greater clarity, support, and consistency.`

## Step 10: Add FAQ Preview

Use a short collapsible section with:

- `How much does it cost to get started?`
- `What happens if I’m not eligible?`
- `How long until I may see visible improvement?`
- `Is Skinfluence a subscription?`

## Step 11: Add Final CTA

Close with:

Headline:
- `A more advanced anti-aging routine starts here.`

Body:
- `Start with evaluation, then move into monthly care if approved.`

CTA:
- `Start Evaluation`

## Section Order Summary

1. Hero
2. Trust section
3. Core problem
4. Premium anti-aging explanation
5. How it works
6. Benefits / who it’s for
7. Why it’s different
8. FAQ preview
9. Final CTA

## Rules While Rebuilding

- keep the storefront evaluation-first, not product-grid-first
- do not surface product prices or product cards on the homepage unless intentionally needed later
- keep major `Start Evaluation` links routed to `/pages/start-evaluation`
- use real imagery where possible
- avoid turning Portland into a fashion storefront with swapped copy

## Recommended Build Sequence

First pass:
- remove demo commerce sections
- keep hero
- rebuild homepage skeleton

Second pass:
- add final copy
- add images

Third pass:
- refine spacing, hierarchy, and image choices

## Decision Rule

If Portland still feels too product-first after this cleanup, then it is the wrong base theme.
If it quickly starts to feel more editorial, calmer, and more premium than the current custom theme, it is the better direction.
