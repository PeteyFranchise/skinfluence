Frequently asked questions
Is tretinoin safe to use at home without seeing a dermatologist in person?
Yes — when prescribed by a licensed physician who has reviewed your skin photos and medical history. Every Skinfluence Protocol subscription includes an asynchronous consultation with a board-certified dermatologist before any prescription is issued. No prescription ships without physician review.
Is this FDA-approved?
Tretinoin is an FDA-approved drug ingredient with over 40 years of clinical research. The compounded formulation — which combines tretinoin with niacinamide and GHK-Cu peptide — is compounded by a PCAB-accredited 503A pharmacy and is not itself FDA-approved, as is the case with all compounded medications. The Tier 1 cosmetic products (GHK-Cu serum, hair peptide treatment) are regulated as cosmetics under the FDA's MoCRA framework.
What if tretinoin irritates my skin?
Retinoid irritation (redness, dryness, peeling) is common in the first 2–6 weeks and is called the "retinization" period. Your physician will start you at a low concentration (typically 0.025%) and titrate up based on your skin's response. The GHK-Cu peptide booster in the formula also helps support skin barrier function during this transition. If you experience significant irritation, your MD is available for an async check-in to adjust your protocol.
Can I cancel my subscription?
Yes. You can pause, adjust, or cancel your subscription at any time through your patient portal. There are no cancellation fees or penalties. We ask that you complete at least one full 30-day cycle before cancelling so your physician can document your response to treatment.
