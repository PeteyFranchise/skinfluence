Critical finding — the research changed the answer
The right choice is Bask for longer than originally planned
A key fact emerged from this research: Bask Health has raised only $760K in total funding with ~40 employees, while OpenLoop has raised $48.5M with 414 employees and just completed a major acquisition. That funding gap matters enormously for a brand betting its clinical infrastructure on a platform's long-term survival. The previous recommendation to migrate at Phase 2 needs to be re-evaluated.
The funding gap is a platform risk, not just a feature comparison. Bask Health has raised $760K total (seed stage, ~40 employees). OpenLoop has raised $48.5M with 414 employees, 5x YoY growth, and a recent acquisition of Season Health (April 2026). This means Bask is a startup building infrastructure that your clinical business will depend on. If Bask struggles financially or pivots, you face a forced migration at the worst possible time — mid-scale, with thousands of patient records. This does not mean avoid Bask. It means plan around the risk deliberately.

Revised phased recommendation — updated for funding reality
Phase 0–1
Months 1–12
Bask Health — no change. Best DTC skincare fit, fastest launch, lowest cost. Negotiate data portability clause upfront. Use your own MD Director (not Bask's network) to keep clinical relationships portable. Document every custom workflow you build in Bask — this reduces migration work later if needed.
Phase 2
Months 13–24
Stay on Bask — but actively monitor. If Bask is serving your 400–2,000 patient volume reliably, there is no business case to migrate. Migration costs $15K–40K and takes 3–5 months — don't pay that unless Bask is genuinely limiting you. Begin a parallel OpenLoop evaluation: run a scoping call, understand their skincare/derm capabilities, and get a contract quote. Keep it in your back pocket.
Phase 3
Months 25–36
Migration trigger point — evaluate three criteria: (1) Are you processing 2,000+ patient visits/month and hitting Bask capacity limits? (2) Do you need OpenLoop's 20,000+ clinician depth for aggressive multi-state expansion? (3) Has Bask shown any financial instability signals (leadership changes, support degradation, pricing hikes)? If two of three are true — migrate to OpenLoop. If not — stay on Bask and allocate the migration budget to patient acquisition instead.
Series A+
Year 3+
OpenLoop is the right long-term partner. At Series A scale (3,000+ subscribers, $250K+ MRR), OpenLoop's managed infrastructure, 20,000+ clinician network, and institutional stability are worth the migration cost. The Season Health acquisition also signals OpenLoop is building toward full-stack virtual care — relevant if Skinfluence expands beyond skin into broader women's health or longevity protocols.
The three migration triggers — only move when 2 of 3 are true
Capacity trigger
You are processing 2,000+ patient consultations per month and hitting queue delays or system performance issues on Bask. Not a concern at Phase 1 volumes — only relevant at scale.
Clinician depth trigger
You need rapid multi-state expansion with board-certified derm coverage in 40+ states simultaneously. OpenLoop's 20,000+ managed clinician network solves this; Bask's partner network may not scale as fast.
Platform stability trigger
Bask shows warning signs: pricing increases above 30%, support response times degrading, leadership changes, or failure to raise a Series A by mid-2026. Watch their funding news closely. A startup at $760K raised is still proving its model.
Expansion trigger
Skinfluence is expanding beyond skin/hair into adjacent protocols (GLP-1, HRT, longevity) where OpenLoop has proven managed infrastructure and OpenLoop's Season Health acquisition adds new capabilities Bask doesn't have.
The smartest move: Start on Bask, negotiate data portability day one, keep your MD Director relationship independent of the platform, and run an OpenLoop scoping call at month 12 — not to migrate, but to have a fully negotiated contract ready to sign within 30 days if a trigger fires. That's the hedge that costs nothing and protects everything.
